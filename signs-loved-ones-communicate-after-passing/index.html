Signs From the Other Side: How Loved Ones Communicate After Passing

After losing someone we love, many of us experience moments that feel like more than coincidence — a song that plays at the perfect moment, a feather appearing in an unlikely place, or a vivid dream that feels like a real visit. Are these signs from our loved ones? Many mediums and spiritual practitioners believe they are.

Why Loved Ones Send Signs

  • To let you know they’re at peace
  • To offer comfort during difficult times
  • To answer a question or provide guidance
  • To mark significant occasions (birthdays, anniversaries)
  • Simply to say “I’m still here, and I love you”

The Most Common Signs

Feathers

Finding feathers in unusual places is one of the most widely reported signs. White feathers, in particular, are associated with messages of love and reassurance from the spirit world.

Songs and Music

Has a specific song — perhaps one connected to your loved one — suddenly played at an unexpected moment? Music is a powerful carrier of spiritual energy, and many people report hearing “their song” during times of need.

Dreams and Visitations

Some dreams feel distinctly different from ordinary ones — more vivid, more real, and more emotionally intense. These “visitation dreams” often feature a deceased loved one appearing healthy, happy, and communicative. Many people wake from these dreams with a deep sense of peace.

Electrical Disturbances

Lights flickering, televisions turning on, or phones behaving strangely around the anniversary of a passing are commonly reported. The theory is that spirit energy can interact with electrical fields.

Animals and Birds

A robin that repeatedly visits your garden, a butterfly that lands on your hand, or a cardinal that appears at meaningful moments — animals have long been considered messengers between the physical and spiritual worlds.

Scents

Smelling your loved one’s perfume, tobacco, cooking, or any distinctive scent when there’s no physical source is a powerful and common sign. It triggers memory so directly.

Coins

Finding coins — especially pennies — in unexpected places is believed by many to be a sign from the other side. The expression “pennies from heaven” reflects this long-held belief.

How to Know It’s a Sign

  • It happens at a meaningful time (during grief, on an anniversary, when you’re thinking of them)
  • It repeats — the same type of sign appears multiple times
  • It’s accompanied by a strong emotional response or a feeling of presence
  • It’s specific to your loved one (their favourite flower, their song, their scent)
  • Your first instinct is “that’s them” — before your logical mind intervenes

What to Do When You Receive a Sign

Simply acknowledge it. You can say aloud or in your heart: “I see you. Thank you. I love you.” There’s no special ritual required — just openness and gratitude.

The Subtle End of the Spectrum

Most signs from people who have crossed over are quiet rather than dramatic. They land in the small moments – a song that comes on at exactly the right time, a coin found in an unlikely place, a flicker of light when you were just thinking of them. People often dismiss these because they’re “too small” or “could be a coincidence.” The rule of thumb among mediums is simple: if it would matter to them, and it lands at a moment that matters to you, it’s almost certainly them.

The other reason these signs feel small is that they don’t disturb our world. The people who have crossed over generally don’t want to frighten us. They want us to feel held, not haunted. So they choose channels that leave room for us to keep doubting if we need to. Belief is always offered, never imposed.

Common Sign Languages

  • Songs and music – especially a particular song that arrives at a particular moment. Music carries emotion well, which makes it a favoured channel.
  • Animals – cardinals, butterflies, dragonflies, robins, certain hawks, or whatever animal had meaning between you. Repeat appearances are the key, not single sightings.
  • Smells – a sudden, unexplained scent of their cologne, perfume, baking, pipe tobacco. Often when no source could possibly be present.
  • Numbers and dates – their birthdate, anniversary, or favourite number appearing in places that catch your attention.
  • Dreams – especially the ones that feel different from ordinary dreams. They tend to be brighter, more present, and you remember them on waking with unusual clarity.
  • Electrical anomalies – lights flickering, the radio briefly playing on its own, devices behaving oddly at the moment you were thinking of them.

Why Some People Receive More Than Others

The single biggest factor isn’t natural psychic ability – it’s permission. People who have given themselves permission to notice tend to receive more than people who unconsciously require absolute proof before they’ll let themselves see anything. The system is gentle. It doesn’t push past skepticism; it meets you exactly where you are.

If you’d like to receive more, you don’t need to do anything elaborate. Just say out loud, or in your own thoughts, that you’re open. That permission alone changes how much you’ll let yourself notice in the weeks that follow.

When the Signs Stop

Many grieving people experience an early flurry of signs in the first weeks or months, then a quieter season. This isn’t abandonment – it’s actually a healthy unfolding. Souls that have crossed over usually want us to integrate, not stay tethered. Once they’ve helped us land, they move closer when we need them and step back when we’re walking forward. The signs grow less frequent because we need them less. They never fully leave.
